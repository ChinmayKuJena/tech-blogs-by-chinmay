PgBouncer Connection Pooling in Docker: Simple Setup

📅 2026-04-05
Learn how a lightweight PgBouncer sidecar can dramatically improve PostgreSQL connection handling in containerized environments.
postgresql docker performance undefined

Insight

Running many short‑lived services (e.g., FastAPI or NestJS) often leads to a flood of PostgreSQL connections, exhausting the DB's max_connections limit and hurting latency. PgBouncer acts as a TCP proxy that reuses a small pool of persistent connections, translating the high churn from the app into a stable load for the database. In Docker, placing PgBouncer in the same network as your app eliminates extra network hops while keeping the DB untouched.

Example

version: "3.9"
services:
  app:
    image: myapp:latest
    environment:
      DATABASE_URL: "postgresql://user:pass@pgbouncer:6432/mydb"
    depends_on:
      - pgbouncer

  pgbouncer:
    image: edoburu/pgbouncer:latest
    environment:
      DB_HOST: postgres
      DB_PORT: 5432
      DB_USER: user
      DB_PASSWORD: pass
      DB_NAME: mydb
    ports:
      - "6432:6432"
    depends_on:
      - postgres

  postgres:
    image: postgres:15-alpine
    environment:
      POSTGRES_USER: user
      POSTGRES_PASSWORD: pass
      POSTGRES_DB: mydb
    volumes:
      - pg_data:/var/lib/postgresql/data
volumes:
  pg_data:

Takeaway

Deploy PgBouncer as a sidecar or separate service, point your application to its port (usually 6432), and tune pool_mode and max_client_conn to match your traffic pattern. This tiny addition can cut connection overhead by 80% and keep your PostgreSQL healthy under load.
