The Over-Indexing Myth: When Too Many Indexes Hurt PostgreSQL Performance

📅 2026-03-21
Why adding indexes indiscriminately can degrade write throughput and increase maintenance cost, and how to audit and prune them effectively.
postgresql performance indexing undefined

Insight

Adding indexes feels like a free performance boost, but each extra index incurs write‑time overhead, consumes disk, and forces the planner to evaluate more paths. In write‑heavy workloads, a handful of well‑chosen indexes outperform a forest of redundant ones. Periodically audit your schema: look for indexes that are never used or duplicate the same column order.

Example

-- Find indexes never used in the last 30 days
SELECT schemaname, relname, indexrelname, idx_scan
FROM pg_stat_user_indexes
WHERE idx_scan = 0
  AND last_vacuum IS NULL
  AND last_analyze IS NULL;

-- Drop an unused index safely
DROP INDEX IF EXISTS idx_orders_customer_id;

Takeaway

Schedule a quarterly index review, keep only indexes that serve frequent read patterns, and watch your write latency improve dramatically.
